Recordkeeping Guidelines for New Life Community Church (508(c)(1)(A) Church)

I. Importance of Recordkeeping for 508(c)(1)(A) Churches
As a 508(c)(1)(A) church, it is critical to maintain detailed and well-organized records. While 508(c)(1)(A) churches are exempt from filing Form 990, thorough recordkeeping is essential to prove your religious purpose and operations to the IRS and state authorities, if needed. Comprehensive records also protect your church in case of any future questions or audits.

II. Required Records
The following records should be maintained by New Life Community Church:

A. Financial Records
- Donation/tithing records
- Bank statements
- Accounting ledgers
- Expense receipts and invoices
- Annual financial statements
- Tax-related documents (e.g., W-2s, 1099s)

Retention Period: Financial records should be kept for at least 7 years.

B. Membership Records
- Membership applications and forms
- Attendance records
- Sacrament records (e.g., baptisms, marriages, funerals)

Retention Period: Membership records should be kept permanently.

C. Meeting Minutes
- Board of Directors/Trustees meetings
- Congregational meetings

Retention Period: Meeting minutes should be kept permanently.

D. Correspondence
- Letters, emails, and other communications with members, donors, and external parties

Retention Period: Correspondence should be kept for at least 3 years.

III. Record Organization and Storage
A. Filing System
Organize records using a standardized filing system, such as the following:
- Financial Records
   - Donation/Tithing Records
   - Bank Statements
   - Accounting Ledgers
   - Expense Records
   - Annual Financial Statements
   - Tax-Related Documents
- Membership Records
   - Membership Applications and Forms
   - Attendance Records
   - Sacrament Records
- Meeting Minutes
   - Board of Directors/Trustees
   - Congregational
- Correspondence
   - Internal
   - External

B. Physical vs. Digital Records
Maintain both physical and digital copies of records whenever possible. Digitize physical documents using a scanner or camera to create backup files.

C. Backup and Security
1. Backup Records:
   - Make regular backups of digital records (e.g., weekly, monthly)
   - Store backup copies in a secure, off-site location (e.g., cloud storage, external hard drive)
2. Secure Physical Records:
   - Store physical records in a locked filing cabinet or safe
   - Limit access to authorized personnel only

IV. Annual Compliance Checklist
Conduct an annual review of your church's recordkeeping practices to ensure compliance with legal requirements and best practices. This should include:
- Reviewing the record retention schedule and making any necessary updates
- Verifying that all required records are being maintained
- Ensuring that backup and security procedures are being followed
- Checking for any state-specific requirements for TX churches

V. State-Specific Requirements for TX
In addition to the general recordkeeping guidelines, churches in Texas should be aware of the following state-specific requirements:
- TX Nonprofit Corporation Act: Requires nonprofits, including churches, to maintain certain records, such as minutes of board and member meetings, and to make these records available for inspection by members.
- TX Property Tax Code: Exempts churches from property taxes, but churches must maintain records to prove their eligibility for this exemption.

VI. Practical Tips for Small Churches
- Assign a dedicated record-keeper or administrative assistant to oversee the church's recordkeeping.
- Invest in a secure, cloud-based storage solution to facilitate digital record-keeping and backup.
- Regularly review and purge outdated records to maintain a well-organized filing system.
- Provide training to staff and volunteers on proper recordkeeping procedures.
- Establish a clear chain of custody for records to ensure continuity during leadership transitions.
