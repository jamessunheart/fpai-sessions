Operating Procedures for New Life Community Church

I. Worship Service Procedures
   A. Service Planning
      1. The worship service schedule and format shall be determined by the Elder Board.
      2. The Elder Board shall appoint a Worship Committee to oversee the planning and execution of worship services.
      3. The Worship Committee shall coordinate with the pastoral staff, music ministry, and other volunteers to ensure all elements of the service are prepared.
   B. Service Execution
      1. Services shall begin and end at the scheduled times.
      2. The order of service shall follow the format approved by the Elder Board.
      3. Ushers shall be responsible for greeting attendees, distributing bulletins, and collecting the offering.
      4. The pastoral staff shall be responsible for delivering the sermon and leading any other liturgical elements.
      5. The music ministry shall be responsible for leading congregational singing and any special music.
   C. Visitor Engagement
      1. Ushers shall be responsible for identifying and welcoming first-time visitors.
      2. Visitor information cards shall be available for guests to provide contact information.
      3. The Membership Committee shall follow up with visitors within one week of their attendance.

II. Financial Management
   A. Offerings
      1. Ushers shall collect the offering during the designated time in the service.
      2. Offering funds shall be counted and recorded by at least two unrelated individuals.
      3. Offering funds shall be deposited into the church's bank account within 24 hours.
      4. Detailed records of all offerings shall be maintained for tax and audit purposes.
   B. Expenses
      1. All expenditures must be approved by the Elder Board or a designated financial committee.
      2. Reimbursement requests must be submitted with proper documentation.
      3. The Treasurer shall maintain accurate records of all income and expenses.
      4. An annual financial audit shall be conducted by an independent third party.
   C. Record-keeping
      1. The Treasurer shall maintain detailed financial records, including bank statements, donation records, and expense receipts.
      2. Financial records shall be kept for a minimum of seven years, as required by the IRS for 508(c)(1)(A) churches.
      3. The church shall file all necessary tax forms, including the annual Form 990-N, to maintain its tax-exempt status.

III. Meeting Procedures
   A. Elder Board Meetings
      1. The Elder Board shall meet weekly to discuss church affairs and make decisions.
      2. A quorum of at least two-thirds of the Elders must be present for any binding votes.
      3. Meeting minutes shall be recorded, reviewed, and approved at the following meeting.
      4. Minutes shall be maintained in the church's permanent records.
   B. Congregational Meetings
      1. An annual congregational meeting shall be held to present the church's financial report and any other important matters.
      2. Special congregational meetings may be called by the Elder Board as needed.
      3. Congregational meetings shall be announced at least two weeks in advance.
      4. A quorum of at least 50% of the church's active members must be present for any binding votes.
      5. Meeting minutes shall be recorded, reviewed, and approved at the following meeting.

IV. Membership Procedures
   A. Joining the Church
      1. Individuals interested in becoming members shall complete a membership application.
      2. The Membership Committee shall review the application and conduct an interview with the applicant.
      3. The Elder Board shall review the Membership Committee's recommendation and vote on the applicant's membership.
   B. Discipline and Removal
      1. Members who engage in unbiblical conduct or doctrine shall be subject to church discipline.
      2. The Elder Board shall follow the procedures outlined in the church's bylaws for discipline and removal of members.
      3. All disciplinary actions shall be documented and maintained in the church's permanent records.

V. Leadership Selection Procedures
   A. Elder Board
      1. Elders shall be nominated and selected according to the qualifications and procedures outlined in the church's bylaws.
      2. The congregation shall have the opportunity to provide input on Elder candidates.
      3. Elders shall be elected by a two-thirds majority vote of the Elder Board.
   B. Other Leadership Positions
      1. Ministry leaders (e.g., worship, youth, outreach) shall be appointed by the Elder Board.
      2. Volunteers for other service roles (e.g., ushers, greeters) shall be recruited and appointed by the appropriate ministry leaders.

VI. Conflict Resolution Procedures
   A. Interpersonal Conflicts
      1. Individuals with conflicts shall first seek to resolve the issue directly with the other party.
      2. If the conflict cannot be resolved, the parties shall involve an Elder or other church leader to mediate the situation.
      3. If the mediation is unsuccessful, the Elder Board shall review the conflict and make a final determination.
   B. Doctrinal or Theological Disputes
      1. Disputes regarding doctrine or theology shall be addressed by the Elder Board.
      2. The Elder Board shall review the issue, consult relevant biblical and theological resources, and make a final determination.
      3. If necessary, the Elder Board may seek guidance from external theological experts.

VII. Amendment Procedures
   A. Proposed Amendments
      1. Any member may propose an amendment to the church's operating procedures.
      2. Proposed amendments must be submitted in writing to the Elder Board for consideration.
   B. Amendment Approval
      1. The Elder Board shall review the proposed amendment and make a recommendation to the congregation.
      2. The proposed amendment shall be presented to the congregation at a special or annual meeting.
      3. The amendment must be approved by a two-thirds majority vote of the active members present at the meeting.
   C. Record-keeping
      1. Approved amendments shall be documented and incorporated into the church's permanent operating procedures.
      2. A history of all amendments shall be maintained for reference.
