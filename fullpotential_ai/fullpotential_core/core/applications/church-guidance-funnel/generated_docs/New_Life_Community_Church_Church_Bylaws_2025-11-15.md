BYLAWS OF NEW LIFE COMMUNITY CHURCH

ARTICLE I: NAME AND PURPOSE

Section 1.1. Name. The name of this religious organization shall be New Life Community Church (hereinafter referred to as the "Church").

Section 1.2. Purpose. The purpose of the Church is to glorify God by worshiping Him, evangelizing the lost, discipling believers, and meeting the spiritual and physical needs of the local community. The Church shall operate as a 508(c)(1)(A) tax-exempt religious organization under the laws of the State of Texas.

ARTICLE II: STATEMENT OF FAITH

Section 2.1. The Holy Scriptures. We believe the Bible, consisting of the sixty-six books of the Old and New Testaments, to be the inspired Word of God, inerrant in the original writings, and the complete revelation of His will for the salvation of mankind. 

Section 2.2. The Godhead. We believe in one God, eternally existing in three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit, each possessing all the attributes of Deity.

Section 2.3. The Lord Jesus Christ. We believe that the Lord Jesus Christ, the eternal Son of God, became man without ceasing to be God, having been conceived by the Holy Spirit and born of the virgin Mary, in order that He might reveal God and redeem sinful humanity.

ARTICLE III: MEMBERSHIP

Section 3.1. Qualifications for Membership. Membership in the Church shall be open to all persons who profess faith in Jesus Christ as their Lord and Savior, have been baptized, and have completed the Church's membership class. 

Section 3.2. Reception of Members. All persons desiring to become members of the Church shall make their desire known to the Lead Pastor or an Elder. Upon the recommendation of the Elders, the Church shall vote on the admission of new members at any regular or special business meeting.

Section 3.3. Responsibilities of Members. Members are expected to support the Church through regular attendance, financial giving, and service. Members shall strive to live in accordance with the Church's Statement of Faith and Biblical principles.

Section 3.4. Discipline of Members. The Elders shall have the authority to discipline members, including temporary suspension or permanent removal from membership, for unrepentant sin or conduct contrary to the Church's Statement of Faith and values.

ARTICLE IV: LEADERSHIP AND OFFICERS

Section 4.1. Board of Elders. The governing body of the Church shall be the Board of Elders, consisting of the Lead Pastor and 3-5 additional Elders. The Elders shall be responsible for the spiritual oversight and direction of the Church.

Section 4.2. Qualifications and Selection of Elders. Elders shall meet the qualifications outlined in 1 Timothy 3:1-7 and Titus 1:5-9. Candidates for Elder shall be nominated by the existing Elders and approved by a two-thirds majority vote of the Church membership.

Section 4.3. Lead Pastor. The Lead Pastor shall be the primary teaching and administrative leader of the Church. The Lead Pastor shall be responsible for overseeing the day-to-day operations of the Church and providing spiritual leadership to the congregation.

Section 4.4. Deacons. The Elders may appoint Deacons to assist in specific ministry areas, such as finance, facilities, or outreach. Deacons shall meet the qualifications outlined in 1 Timothy 3:8-13.

ARTICLE V: MEETINGS

Section 5.1. Worship Services. The Church shall hold regular weekly worship services for the purpose of glorifying God through music, prayer, preaching, and fellowship.

Section 5.2. Business Meetings. The Church shall hold an annual business meeting, as well as any special business meetings deemed necessary by the Elders. A quorum of 50% of the active membership shall be required to conduct business.

Section 5.3. Voting. All active members in good standing shall be eligible to vote on matters presented at business meetings. Unless otherwise specified in these Bylaws, all decisions shall be made by a simple majority vote.

ARTICLE VI: FINANCES

Section 6.1. Financial Stewardship. The Elders shall be responsible for the financial stewardship of the Church, including the development and oversight of the annual budget.

Section 6.2. Designated Gifts. The Church may accept designated gifts, provided they are consistent with the Church's mission and values. The Elders shall have the final authority to accept or decline any designated gifts.

Section 6.3. Reporting. An annual financial report shall be presented to the congregation at the annual business meeting.

ARTICLE VII: AMENDMENTS

Section 7.1. Bylaw Amendments. These Bylaws may be amended by a two-thirds majority vote of the active Church membership at any regular or special business meeting, provided that the proposed amendment has been made available to the congregation at least two weeks prior to the meeting.

ARTICLE VIII: DISSOLUTION

Section 8.1. Dissolution. In the event of the dissolution of the Church, all assets shall be distributed to one or more recognized 501(c)(3) or 508(c)(1)(A) tax-exempt organizations whose purpose and activities are consistent with those of the Church.
